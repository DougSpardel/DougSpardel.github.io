Background

Economics encompasses a plethora of topics and I found it important to specialize and find the areas suited for me. I initially was convinced that I would make an excellent lawyer and policy maker, but was quickly enamored with pricing, optimaization, and overall marginal analysis due to the mathematical applications. This track led me to hunt for the root of optimization which is ultiamtely the study of Game Theory, where I was able to focus on COVID-19 related topics such as the introduction of ventilators and the idea of grey-area cases being used as political fuel. Ultimately, I became frustrated with the theoretical nature of game theory, thus, evolving my interests to return to statistics and enhance my understanding of the world through causal regressions. Combining game theory and data analysis, I came to the understanding that my true interests did not strictly lie in explaining the present, but in predicting the future. In my recent applied microeconometrics class, I was able to master STATA and move on to my ultimate goal of learning Machine Learning with Python. My recent studies have involved analyzing the future of immigration flow over the Mexican border using past trends.

Keeping an open mind and learning out of interest rather than making a schedule easier or taking shortcuts has always been my philosophy. This mindset has allowed me to apply applications to the real world whether it be choosing the most ethical policy, optimal price, or beneficial decision for the individual.
